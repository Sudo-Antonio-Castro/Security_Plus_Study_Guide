Policies, Standards, and Procedures

Policies define intent, standards define required rules, and procedures describe how work is actually performed.

Core Ideas

  • Governance aligns security work to legal, regulatory, and business expectations.
  • Different documents exist for different levels of control and flexibility.

Support Focus

  • Do not confuse a policy with a step-by-step procedure.
  • Governance should be understandable enough to guide decisions during change and incident response.
Summary

Well-written governance documents reduce ambiguity before problems happen.

Security+ Exam Tip

Policies are broad, standards are mandatory specifics, and procedures explain how to do the work.

Change Management

Change management reduces outage and security risk by reviewing, approving, documenting, and validating changes before production impact grows.

Core Ideas

  • Production changes should include rollback planning, testing, approvals, and timing.
  • Emergency changes may be faster but still require documentation and review.

Support Focus

  • Security changes can introduce risk if applied without coordination.
  • Auditability matters as much as speed in regulated environments.
Summary

Good change control protects availability and accountability at the same time.

Security+ Exam Tip

A backout plan is one of the most practical details in any change scenario.

Automation and Orchestration

Automation executes repeatable tasks while orchestration coordinates multiple automated and manual steps across tools or teams.

Core Ideas

  • Scripts, workflows, playbooks, and APIs can improve speed and consistency.
  • Automation should reduce toil without removing needed oversight from high-risk decisions.

Support Focus

  • Use automation where actions are frequent, well understood, and low ambiguity.
  • Document assumptions and permissions for every automated process.
Summary

The best automation improves reliability, not just speed.

Security+ Exam Tip

If a question mentions connecting several tools into one workflow, orchestration is likely the better term.
