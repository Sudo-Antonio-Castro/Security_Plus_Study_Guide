Cryptographic Algorithms

Security+ expects you to know what symmetric encryption, asymmetric encryption, and hashing each do best.

Core Ideas

  • Symmetric encryption is fast and ideal for bulk confidentiality.
  • Asymmetric encryption supports key exchange, identity, and signatures but costs more in processing.

Support Focus

  • Hashing is one-way and supports integrity verification.
  • Key length influences brute-force resistance and performance trade-offs.
Summary

Choose cryptographic primitives based on confidentiality, integrity, authentication, and scale.

Security+ Exam Tip

Hashing is not encryption, and encryption is not a digital signature.

Public Key Infrastructure

PKI establishes trust in public keys by binding them to identities through digital certificates and certificate authorities.

Core Ideas

  • Certificates carry public keys plus identity information and validation metadata.
  • Certificate authorities, chains of trust, revocation, and lifecycle management are central PKI ideas.

Support Focus

  • Know the roles of root CAs, intermediates, CSRs, and certificate stores.
  • A certificate proves identity only if the issuing CA is trusted.
Summary

PKI makes public key cryptography practical at organizational and Internet scale.

Security+ Exam Tip

If trust in the CA breaks down, trust in the certificate breaks down too.

Cryptographic Solutions

Real environments combine primitives into protocols and products such as TLS, VPNs, code signing, and encrypted storage.

Core Ideas

  • Cipher suites combine key exchange, encryption, hashing, and signing methods.
  • Key storage, rotation, escrow, and secure implementation matter as much as algorithm choice.

Support Focus

  • Use digital signatures for authenticity and integrity.
  • Use encryption in transit and at rest to match the sensitivity of the data.
Summary

Strong cryptography fails quickly when keys are mishandled or weak protocols are chosen.

Security+ Exam Tip

Perfect forward secrecy limits damage if a long-term key is later exposed.
